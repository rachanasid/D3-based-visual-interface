Analyzing and Visualizing UK Road Accidents 2015

        
  • Road traffic injuries can affect all populations, regardless of age, sex, or geographic region.
    •     
  • The economic cost of the problem in Britain is in the region of £13 billion a year (Department for Transport).
    •    
             
  • With more vehicles on the road than ever before, its important to understand them in greater detail.
    •     
  • The data includes generic and specific details about the vehicles, driver,severity of accidents.

Correlation Matrix

Gives the correlation between the different factors responsible for road accidents.

Scatterplot Matrix

Most of the accidents occur when there is no special condition at site (blue represents none), followed by special condition roadworks i.e when there is an on going road work (green represents roadworks). From vehicle location we can infer that, most of the accidents occur on main carriageway (1 represents main carriageway), followed by the vehicle location Footway (10 represents footway - pavement).

Weekly Trend of Accidents

The histogram displays the frequency of accidents in each month of 2015. The pie chart displays the frequency of accidents for each month.

Inference : It can be inferred that the number of accidents are high on Friday (for most of them months) because people usually tend to go for a vacation as it is the beginning of the weekend.

Accident Severity when weather is fine with no winds

The donut chart displays the severity of the accidents when the weather condition is Fine no high winds.
Inference :We can infer that when the weather is fine and without wind, rain or snow, the accident severity is likely to be Slight.

Severity Based on Point Of Contact

The bar chart displays the frequency of Fatal accidents versus first point of impact .
Inference : It can be inferred that if the first point of impact is Front, then the number of Fatal accidents would be high.

Age Group Vs Number of Accidents

This barchart shows the distribution of age groups versus the number of accidents. The pink bars represent number of female drivers while the blue bars represent number of male drivers. The bars overlap to show the difference in frequency of the accidents.
Inference : The maximum number of accidents are committed by the age band 26-45 irrespective of the gender. This is expected as the young adults travel more.
Top 5 Most Populous Districts : Birmingham, Leeds, Sheffield, Cornwall, Bradford
Top 5 Most Places by Accidents : Birmingham, Leeds, Westminer, Cornwall, Liverpool
The Treemap represents the number of accidents occuring in UK in the top 5 districts. Each district is again subdivided based on the weather conditions when the accident occured.
Inference : We are able to infer that most number of accidents occur in highly populated places. We are also able to infer that the number of accidents are high when the weather is 'Fine no high wind' irrespective of the district.

Home Area Type Versus Severity Of the Accidents

Home Area can be broadly classified into Rural area, Small town and Urban Area. A group bar chart best describes the severity of the accidents occuring at these places. Log Scale has been applied on the data for better visualization.
Inference : The following can be inferred from the group bar chart :
  • Most of the accidents have taken place in Urban areas. This is intuitive as the population is more in urban areas as compared to other areas.
  • It can also be inferred that the Fatal accidents are more in Urban areas.
